# Cognition And Planning

## Scope

Use this reference when the user asks about planning, future direction, emotion regulation, cognitive improvement, decision-making, or applying scientific method to life.

## Framing

Treat planning and emotion regulation as systems with feedback, not as willpower problems alone.

Useful mappings:

- goal: desired state or direction,
- state: current energy, attention, constraints, environment, and commitments,
- input: actions, routines, information, conversations, rest, and tools,
- feedback: mood, progress, friction, avoidance, errors, body signals, and external results,
- noise: temporary emotional spikes, social pressure, and misleading metrics,
- delay: results that appear after days, weeks, or months.

## Teaching Guidance

1. Avoid over-psychologizing the learner.
2. Do not store single emotional events as stable profile facts.
3. Use control, feedback, uncertainty, and system-boundary language.
4. Distinguish signals from noise.
5. Ask for the learner's own formulation before making strong inferences.

## Useful Questions

- What state is the learner trying to stabilize or change?
- What feedback signal shows whether the strategy works?
- Is the feedback immediate or delayed?
- Is this signal or temporary noise?
- What can be changed: environment, routine, information, incentives, or interpretation?
- Is the problem local, or does it come from the larger system boundary?
